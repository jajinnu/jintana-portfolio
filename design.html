Design




This section demonstrates my mastery of industry-leading design tools, highlighting how each has shaped my ability to deliver cutting-edge solutions across various projects. Whether crafting imaginative 3D visuals or refining product designs, I leverage these tools to bring innovative concepts to life while maintaining a high standard of technical excellence.

Blender

Blender, an open-source 3D modeling powerhouse, is my go-to tool for creating visually captivating and intricate models. With its robust feature set, I push the boundaries of my creativity, turning abstract ideas into tangible, high-quality visuals. My Blender work reflects not only technical know-how but also the power of storytelling through design. Here are some key projects I've completed using Blender:

Cartoon Vending Machine: Designed a playful, cartoon-style vending machine, emphasizing creativity and vibrant aesthetics. This project showcases my ability to produce engaging and imaginative 3D visuals. (Image (1/3))

Directional Sign:Developed for my thesis, featuring painted textures to illustrate two different paths—a straight route and a curved path surrounded by trees. This demonstrates my skills in texture painting and environmental modeling.(Image (2/3))

Character Design: Created a character inspired by a cactus and eggs, with vivid colors and dynamic lighting. This project highlights my ability to craft unique and lively 3D characters, enhancing visual appeal and storytelling.(Image (3/3))

SketchUp

SketchUp is my preferred tool for architectural and product design, thanks to its intuitive interface and robust capabilities. I use it to translate 2D sketches into detailed 3D models that are functional, aesthetically pleasing, and practical for both client presentations and production workflows. Here’s how I’ve utilized it:

Dream House Design: Starting from a conceptual floor plan, I used SketchUp to create a detailed 3D model of a dream house, which includes multiple rooms and functional spaces. This project demonstrates my ability to think spatially and convert architectural concepts into realistic designs, making it ideal for interior planning and architectural presentations. I optimized my workflow with SketchUp’s extensive component library, ensuring efficiency while maintaining creativity.

Fusion360

Fusion 360 is an essential tool in my design arsenal for CAD/CAM projects, enabling me to create precise, functional product designs. It bridges the gap between artistic creativity and engineering functionality, allowing me to develop products that are as technically sound as they are visually appealing.

Stamp Holder: For a Smart Environment project, I designed a custom rubber stamp holder, utilizing Fusion 360’s parametric modeling tools to ensure precision. I further enhanced the design with Paint 3D to visualize colors and shadows, demonstrating my ability to combine engineering accuracy with aesthetic considerations.

Smart Glasses:I developed a concept for smart glasses aimed at improving communication through Morse code. Fusion 360 allowed me to create a complex, yet functional design, pushing my boundaries in product innovation. This project highlights my ability to adapt quickly to new tools and explore product usability in the tech-driven world.

Maya

Autodesk Maya is my primary tool for more intricate 3D modeling and animation work. Its powerful features help me create highly detailed and immersive environments, making it an excellent choice for complex models and interactive applications. Here’s what I’ve created with it:

Arcade Machine: In our M4 project, we developed an arcade machine to raise awareness about accepting cookies online. It starts off user-friendly, but as participants interact with the dance mat, the experience turns unsettling, revealing that by answering questions, they’ve given their consent—similar to online cookie practices.

I contributed by designing a 3D model of the machine using Maya, which helped visualize its placement in a room and finalize the layout before construction. The project was showcased at the Gogbot festival in Enschede, where the real installation (as seen in the image below) won an award for our innovative approach to privacy awareness.

Moon Landing Snow Globe: I designed a snow globe with a detailed moon landing scene, including astronauts and a moon lander. The model, imported into Unity, reflects both detailed modeling and potential for interactive features. This project showcases my ability to combine creativity with technical precision in 3D environments.
