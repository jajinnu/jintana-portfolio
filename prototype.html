Word clock project

Inspired by a word clock I saw at a friend's house, I decided to design my own version. The process began with hand-drawn sketches and quickly progressed to a cardboard prototype, incorporating an Arduino Nano for real-time functionality. I programmed the LED lights to update the time via an internet connection, integrating the system seamlessly with the house's network.

To refine the design, I attended a laser-cutting workshop, using Inkscape to create the final design. With access to a design lab, I transformed the concept into a polished, laser-cut model. Unlike traditional word clocks that display time in words (e.g., "quarter to five"), I customized my version to show time numerically, improving readability while maintaining the original concept.

Candle Holder project

For this project, I designed a distinctive candle holder inspired by the playful form of a hedgehog. Using Blender, I crafted a detailed 3D model with a unique spiked texture and incorporated four sturdy legs for stability. After finalizing the design, I 3D printed the holder and tested it with actual candles, ensuring that the dimensions were precisely aligned for proper functionality.

The combination of the spiked texture and the robust four-legged design highlights my attention to detail and demonstrates my ability to translate 3D models into functional, real-world objects. This project not only showcases my skills in creative design and practical implementation but also underscores my proficiency in both 3D modeling and physical prototyping.

Key highlights of the project include:

Innovative Design: The spiked texture not only adds visual interest but also reflects my ability to create engaging and whimsical designs.

Functional Features: The four-legged base ensures the candle holder stands securely, demonstrating my focus on practical usability alongside creative aesthetics.

Precise Execution: After finalizing the Blender model, I 3D printed the holder and carefully measured the candle size to guarantee a perfect fit. This attention to detail ensures the prototype functions as intended in a real-world setting.

3D Lamp Installation

For a high school assignment on "Everyday Objects," I created a unique LED lamp installation inspired by the TWIX bar—a snack I frequently encountered in the vending machine. Opting for a 3D approach allowed me to explore the potential of transforming a familiar item into an interactive art piece.

This project highlights my foundational skills in 3D modeling and functional art, demonstrating my enthusiasm for creating innovative and interactive design solutions.

Concept & Design:The installation features tubes representing the layers of a TWIX bar, symbolizing its distinct ingredients. I bent straight steel wire to form the structure, incorporating three tubes in between, each filled with beads to represent the colors and textures of the TWIX filling.

Technical Execution: LED wires were integrated into the design, providing functional lighting controlled by a switch. This demonstrated my ability to combine artistic vision with practical functionality, despite my developing technical skills at the time.

Creative Exploration: This project highlights my early interest in interactive installations and 3D design. It illustrates my capacity to merge creative concepts with practical elements, resulting in a visually engaging and innovative design solution.
